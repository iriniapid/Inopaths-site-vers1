    Concept

    The Interactive Decarbonisation Simulator (IDS) aims at providing an intuitive insight into decarbonisation strategies in order to facilitate stakeholder engagement. The tool will help understanding the mechanics of decarbonisation and the implications of modifying effort-sharing choices and other factors for the Member States and the EU. The IDS aims at replicating the projection properties of the PRIMES model (country by country for the EU Member States and until 2050) while inviting users to interact with exogenous assumptions and visualising the implications for the decarbonisation pathways. By designing a few different decarbonisation strategies, the user can quickly get an intuitive understanding of which measures have a large effect and which don't, and where potential bottlenecks might lie.

    Backend design

    The backend model development of the Prototype Interactive Decarbonisation Simulator (IDS) is based on PRIMES. PRIMES is a partial equilibrium modelling system that simulates an energy market equilibrium in the European Union and in each of its Member States. The model includes consistent EU carbon price trajectories. It proceeds in five-year steps and uses Eurostat data.

    A note about the EUCO scenarios

    As part of the European Commission's Impact Assessment work in 2016, two core policy scenarios, several scenarios were built, with the EU Reference Scenario 2016 as a starting point. They model the achievement of the 2030 climate and energy targets as agreed by the European Council in 2014 .The main scenario, EUCO27 considers at least 40% cuts in greenhouse gas emissions (from 1990 levels), at least 27% share for renewable energy and at least 27% improvement in energy efficiency. Four variants to the EUCO27, considering different levels of energy efficiency improvements (30, 33, 35 and 40%) have been also considered to assess the impact of the proposed legal framework on energy efficiency.

    CO2 emissions from energy are computed by multiplying quantities of fossil fuel combusted (measures in energy terms) by emission factors, which are specific to fuels.

    RES share as % of gross final energy is handled by PRIMES as a target, either by sector, country or EU-wide. Feed-in tariff and other supporting schemes for renewables are included.

    Energy savings are one of the possible combination of inputs that can be used for any given technology. These may include direct energy saving measures, but also other techniques (e.g. heat recovery). Energy efficiency measures include the use of more efficient equipment, insulation of buildings, control systems in industry etc.
